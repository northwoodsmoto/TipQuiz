1. Permit Facts Dom Cannot Ignore

  • Eligibility: ≥16 yrs, valid Michigan driver license, vision test + written knowledge test.
  • Under 18: parent/guardian signature and proof of safety-course enrollment required.
  • Fee: $16 (TIP) — can be paid at any SOS branch.
  • Validity: 180 days. Max 2 TIPs in any 10-year window.
  • Restrictions:
    • No passengers (sorry, Olivia—date night waits).
    • No riding between ½ hour after sunset → ½ hour before sunrise.
    • Constant visual supervision by a CY-endorsed rider ≥18 (they can ride separate bike or be in a car).
Remember: Violating TIP restrictions = ticket ➜ permit void ➜ mandatory waiting period before retest.

2. Equipment & Michigan-Specific Legal Stuff

Helmet Law (MCL 257.658)

  • Under 21: DOT-approved helmet always.
  • 21+ may skip the lid only if BOTH:
    • $20 k first-party medical-benefits insurance, and
    • CY endorsement ≥ 2 years or completed approved safety course.
  • Passengers follow same rule set.

Required Equipment (road-legal)

  • Head-lamp (always on for 1973+ bikes).
  • At least one rear-view mirror (left or right).
  • Red tail/brake light, white plate light.
  • Horn audible ≥200 ft.
  • Wheel fenders, decent muffler (≤86 dBA urban-test).
  • Handlebars: grips ≤15 in above seat.

Insurance & Documents

  • Carry proof of insurance card + registration.
  • Police may request to see TIP and license; failure = civil infraction.

4. Core Riding Skills Tested in Questions

Starting, Stopping, Shifting

  • Clutch friction zone = feather power for low-speed balance.
  • Downshift before stops; remain in 1st at lights for escape.
  • Quick stop = both brakes, progressive squeeze, keep bike upright.

Cornering Formula

  1. Slow (brake) before curve.
  2. Look through the turn (head & eyes).
  3. Press inside grip to lean.
  4. Roll on throttle smoothly to stabilize.

Swerving

  • Two quick, counter-steer presses—Press → Press.
  • Do not brake while swerving (upright first, then brake if needed).

5. Traffic Strategy & Lane Positioning

  • Intersections = #1 crash site. Cover clutch & brakes, weave brake-light, create escape gap.
  • Lane positions (left-center-right tire tracks): pick based on visibility, wind, surfaces—not habit.
  • Following distance: 2 sec min (4 sec ideal) behind vehicles; double in rain/gravel.
  • SIPDE: Scan → Identify → Predict → Decide → Execute.
  • Blind zones: get out quickly; don’t pace side-by-side.
  • Group rides: staggered except single-file in curves or hazards; only one bike passes at a time.

6. Hazards & Emergency Handling

Surface Hazards

  • Potholes/bumps: rise on pegs, relax grip, throttle steady.
  • Rail tracks/bridge gratings: cross at 45–90° when safe, stay perpendicular if wet.
  • Gravel & sand: smooth inputs, light rear brake, keep arms loose.
  • Rain’s first 15 minutes = oil-film skate rink.

Mechanical Failures

  • Tire blow-out: steady throttle, no front brake, gradual rear brake, steer off road.
  • Throttle stuck: cut ignition (kill-switch), clutch in, brake.
  • Wobble/shimmy: firm knees, steady throttle, shift weight forward, then slow gradually.

7. Impairment & Fatigue Facts Dom Must Quote

  • Alcohol: hits brain in 20-30 sec; judgment drops before coordination.
  • BAC .08% illegal for any vehicle, but impairment starts at .02%.
  • 1 drink/hour = only rough guideline; weight, food, fatigue change effects.
  • Mixing Rx meds (e.g., antihistamines, opioids) can multiply drowsiness.
  • Fatigue: 18 hrs awake ≈ BAC 0.05 for reaction time.

8. From TIP to Full CY Endorsement

  1. Option A: Complete approved Michigan Rider Education Program (MI-REP) Basic Rider Course ➜ skills test waived.
  2. Option B: Schedule on-cycle skills test with 3rd-party examiner within permit’s 180 days.
  3. Pass either ➜ SOS adds “CY” to license ➜ Olivia finally gets her back-seat upgrade.

9. Memory Hacks & Test-Day Tips

  • Put the numbers on repeat: 180 days, 2 TIPs/10 yrs, 4-sec following, 20 k insurance, .08 BAC.
  • Flash cards: write hazard scenarios on one side, correct response on other.
  • SIPDE shout-out: In your car, narrate hazards aloud on daily drives.
  • Sleep & hydrate: retention is 40 % better after 7-8 hrs sleep.
  • Test mode: answer sure bets first, flag tough ones, revisit with fresh eyes.
